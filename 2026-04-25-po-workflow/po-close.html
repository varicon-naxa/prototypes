Why a soft prompt instead of hard auto-close? Civil construction often has late retention drawdowns, credits, or last-minute adjustments. A surprise close can block legitimate bills and frustrate the AP team. The soft prompt gives the user explicit choice without requiring them to navigate to the PO to mark it closed.

Open decision: if no action is taken, should the prompt resurface daily, weekly, or only re-trigger when activity resumes? Recommended: re-surface after 30 days of no new bills.

Why distinguish cancel from close? Closed = work was completed (perhaps under-delivered). Cancelled = work never happened. Reporting and supplier metrics need this distinction so a never-actioned PO doesn't pollute "completion" KPIs.

Lighter approval: creator + one approver in the chain (vs full re-approval). The deal was already approved at this value — cancellation just acknowledges it won't proceed.
